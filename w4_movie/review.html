Review

Ethan Paul - 4/17/2026


The SpongeBob SquarePants Movie was such an amazing movie, though I may be biased. I remember growing up watching SpongeBob everyday--I can very accurately recall almost any scene from any episode up to a certain point. While I feel as though its age definitely holds it back from making use of more modern animation techniques that can be found in the more recent Spongebob movies, its age also gives this movie its charm. The SpongeBob SquarePants Movie was the intended ending to the animated series--an early look into the future of my favorite underwater character--however, it brings me such mixed emotions to think of how that could've been it. In between the masterful animation, simple humor, the sense of adventure and tension, and the overarching themes of maturity and worth exists a finale. This is the way SpongeBob was supposed to hang up his spatula for younger audiences--succeeding in the resturant industry and growing up from being a "boy" and becoming a "man". I genuinely enjoyed seeing him get what he deserved as he brought me so much joy throughout my childhood, though he still brings me joy now as SpongeBob is the only show I watch on television.


Now, onto a genuine breakdown of The SpongeBob SquarePants Movie itself. The plot was honestly a bit rushed, with Plankton's lemon-scented plan "z" suddenly being the exact remedy to his constant loss streak against Krabs--bringing imbalance to their usual dynamic. Despite this, as SpongeBob episodes are typically chaotic, it had a sense of familiarity. Additionally, the idea that Mr. Krabs would've thought of expanding his business and not putting SpongeBob in a management position is insane, that sponge has been employee of the month for like 300 or more consecutive months--much more than even the most dedicated employees can possibly achieve. This brought into check SpongeBob's moral character and gave way to the exploration of the themes of the movie. He originally planned to abandon Mr. Krabs--it was his mouthing off that led to Krabs being frozen by King Neptune--but he didn't. He stepped up when it mattered and when everyone else thought that there was no possible way he would be able to succeed (that's courage if you ask me). Through ups and downs full of jokes and tension, while being chased down by a literal bounty hunter, SpongeBob and Patrick explore what it means to be men. It is more than silly seaweed moustaches plastered on their faces by Princess Mindy, it is stepping up to unwanted responsibilities and facing them with bravery. The SpongeBob SquarePants Movie gave SpongeBob a depth in his character that was previously unseen--it allowed children to see that in the face of oppression and in the lack of support, they may still flourish.


The SpongeBob SquarePants Movie had an incredible soundtrack, iconic in more than one way. It added onto the already layered scenes--ones of happiness and ones of sadness. I will always and forever be a goofy goober. Excellent movie. ★★★★★
